Hold the Line Privacy Policy

Last updated: 13 June 2026

The short version: Hold the Line does all of its analysis on your device. It does not collect, store, transmit, or share your camera images, microphone audio, or any personal data. There are no servers, no accounts, and no analytics.

What Hold the Line does

Hold the Line is a party game. It reads facial expressions and behavioral cues from your device's camera and (optionally) microphone in real time to power a live "composure / tells" meter, so players can try to read — and keep — a poker face. It is a game, not a lie detector: the meter shows how calm or expressive someone looks, not whether anything they say is true. A calm liar wins.

Camera and microphone

  • Camera: Used only to analyze the active player's expressions live, on your device. Video frames are processed in the moment and are never recorded, saved, or sent anywhere. The brief snapshots shown in the end-of-match recap are held in memory only and are gone when you close the app.
  • Microphone (optional): If you turn the mic on, audio is analyzed on-device for pitch and pause patterns only. Audio is never recorded, saved, or transmitted. The mic is off by default and you control it.

Data we store on your device

The only data Hold the Line saves is each player's baseline profile (numeric cue averages, not images or audio), kept in your browser's local storage so players don't have to re-calibrate every round. You can erase it any time by clearing the app's site data. This information never leaves your device.

Data we collect

None. Hold the Line has no backend servers, no user accounts, no advertising, no third-party analytics, and no tracking. We cannot see any of your data because it never reaches us.

Children

Hold the Line is not directed at children under 13 and collects no personal information from anyone.

Consent and responsible use

This is a game played among people who have opted in and can see the camera. You are still responsible for getting the consent of everyone on screen and for complying with the recording and privacy laws where you live.
